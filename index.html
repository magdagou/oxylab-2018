THE OPIOID EPIDEMIC

In the 1990s, healthcare providers began to heavily prescribe Opioids. This surge in pain relief prescriptions resulted in an increase in the drugâ€™s misuse. In 2016, 116 people died of opioid overdoses daily. Consequences of these highly addictive drugs are prenatal use leading to infant withdrawal, organ malfunction, and fatal overdose. Although every demographic is affected by the epidemic, young adults are the most at risk due to their need to relieve stress, pain, as well as their desire to experiment. Unfortunately, opioid death rates continue to climb to this day.
